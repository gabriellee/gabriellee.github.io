owlBits!

This past summer I worked in the Lifelong Kindergarten group at the MIT Media Lab under Dr. Amon Milner, crafting lessons to teach neuroscience using littleBits, a DIY electronics platform. The lesson is designed to make neuroscience accessible to students with a wide range of learning styles. Self-directed lessons allow students to proceed at their own pace, making it less embarrassing for students who might require more time. Click here to see the full lesson plan.

Synthesizer

I worked on a four person team to create a digital and analog musical synthesizer. The synthesizer takes in a midi signal and extracts pitch and duration. This information is translated into a square wave with correct frequency on an arduino. An ASDR filter modifies the output. Click here to see the website for it.
